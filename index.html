My parents were refugees from Vietnam and they traveled to California by boat which was hard for them. My mom found work at a nail salon and my dad was in construction. My parents were born in Vietnam in the 1970s and because it was impossible to find jobs or buy a home, they moved to California to have a better life for themselves and their future family. Since I grew up in a culture different from mine, I have more expectations placed on me. My parents have taught me to pursue a higher education through good schools. I was placed in an ESL class which helped me learn English and finally be able to communicate with my peers. Soon I became my parent's translator. I had to explain to my parents what was happening at school and had to translate whenever the landlord or banks called. This taught me how to be patient and resourceful not because I wanted to but because I had to. Because my parents don't know anything about the application process, this club has given me so much support and guidance.
